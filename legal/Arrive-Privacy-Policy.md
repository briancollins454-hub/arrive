# Privacy Policy — Arrivé

**Last updated: 7 June 2026**

---

## 1. Who we are

Arrivé is a hotel management and direct booking platform operated by
**Brian Sinclair trading as The Supports Desk** ("Arrivé", "we", "us"), based at
**39 Rathmore Heights, Ballymena, BT43 6NQ**.

For data protection questions, contact **brian@thesupportsdesk.com**.

> **Note on your two roles.** This is important and often missed. Arrivé acts in
> two capacities:
> - As a **data controller** for your own business contacts, the hotel staff who
>   hold accounts, and visitors to your marketing site.
> - As a **data processor** for the guest data that hotels collect through the
>   booking engine. The hotel is the controller of its guests' data; you process
>   it on their behalf. This means each hotel needs a **Data Processing Agreement
>   (DPA)** with you. A DPA template is the companion document to this policy and
>   you should not onboard a paying hotel without one signed.

## 2. What data we collect

**From hotel staff (account holders):** name, email address, role, and login
credentials (passwords are stored hashed by our authentication provider, never in
plain text).

**From guests making bookings (processed on behalf of the hotel):** name, email,
phone number, booking dates, room preferences, special requests, and payment
confirmation references. **We do not store full card numbers** — card payments are
handled by Stripe and we retain only a payment reference token.

**Automatically:** basic technical data such as IP address, browser type, and usage
logs, for security and to keep the service running.

## 3. How we use it and our legal basis

| Purpose | Legal basis (UK GDPR Art. 6) |
|---|---|
| Operating hotel accounts and the platform | Contract |
| Processing guest bookings on a hotel's behalf | Processor acting on controller's basis |
| Taking payment | Contract |
| Security, fraud prevention, logs | Legitimate interests |
| Marketing emails to hotels (not guests) | Consent / legitimate interests |
| Legal and tax record-keeping | Legal obligation |

We do **not** sell personal data. We do not use guest data for our own marketing.

## 4. Payments

Card payments are processed by **Stripe**, a PCI-DSS Level 1 certified provider.
Card details are entered directly into Stripe's secure systems and are never seen
or stored by Arrivé. Stripe's own privacy terms apply to that processing:
https://stripe.com/privacy

## 5. Who we share data with (sub-processors)

We use a small number of trusted providers to run the service. Each is bound to
protect the data:

- **Supabase** — database and authentication hosting, stored in the **EU (Ireland),
  eu-west-1** region
- **Vercel** — application hosting
- **Stripe** — payment processing
- **Resend** — email delivery for booking confirmations and notifications
- **Anthropic** — only if the optional AI assistant add-on is enabled by the hotel;
  guest data sent to the AI feature is processed to generate responses and is not
  used to train models under Anthropic's commercial terms

All guest and account data is stored in the **EU (Ireland)** region (`eu-west-1`).
This location is within the UK adequacy area, so no additional transfer safeguard
(such as Standard Contractual Clauses) is required.

## 6. How long we keep it

We keep guest booking data for as long as the hotel's account is active and as
required for the hotel to run its business and meet its legal obligations. When a
hotel leaves Arrivé, its data is deleted or returned within **30 days**,
except where we must retain limited records for legal or tax reasons.

## 7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to
the processing of your personal data, and to data portability. **Guests** should
contact the hotel they booked with in the first instance (the hotel is the
controller); we will support the hotel in responding. To exercise rights directly
with us, email **brian@thesupportsdesk.com**. You also have the right to complain to the
**Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)** at ico.org.uk.

## 8. Security

We protect data with encryption in transit, row-level database security isolating
each hotel's data, restricted access controls, and secrets held server-side and
never exposed to browsers. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable and
appropriate measures and review them regularly.
